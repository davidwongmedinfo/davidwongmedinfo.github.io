Hi there!

I am David (Dawei) Wong, a researcher and engineer in medical bioinformatics. With a Bachelor's and Master's degree in Medicine, I am concurrently pursuing a second Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science while actively engaged in my professional endeavors.

Determined to delve deeply into the medical field with unwavering commitment, my undergraduate years were marked by a unique and unforgettable full-time clinical internship that has profoundly inspired me ever since, solidifying my dedication to this field and promising to continue on this path. During my Master's studies, I gained valuable experience in transcriptome analysis, single-cell analysis and biomedical modeling, which has significantly enriched my research capabilities. As I embark on my second Bachelor's program, I am endeavoring to expand my research scope to medical AI, particularly focusing on deep learning-driven anti-tumor drug design.

My research interests encompass: (a) Developing tumor markers and risk prediction models based on multi-omics analysis; (b) Applying deep learning in healthcare big data, such as AI-assisted diagnosis and treatment. Furthermore, I find it exhilarating to explore new research areas and am always receptive to new challenges. With my interdisciplinary educational backgrounds, I aspire to contribute groundbreaking research that harnesses cutting-edge informatics technology to address complex diseases like cancer, thereby advancing personalized, precision medicine.

Beyond my specialized studies, I maintain a broad range of passions spanning physics, electronic engineering and 3D modeling and animation (please explore them in the 'Gallery' section if they you are interested). I have been continuously dedicating my free time to deepening my understanding and refining my skills within these fascinating domains.

I am looking for a Ph.D. position in computational biology/bioinformatics starting from Fall 2025. Please feel free to reach out if you are interested.
